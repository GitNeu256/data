Hololive Productions is a virtual YouTuber agency owned by Japanese high-tech entertainment company Cover Inc. In addition to its role as a multi-channel network, Hololive Productions is also involved in merchandising, especially in music production and concert organization. as of January 2023, the agency manages 75 VTubers in three target languages (Japanese, Indonesian and English) and has a total of over 50 million registered users, including the most registered VTuber on YouTube.
The name "Hololive" was initially used for Cover's 3D streaming app launched in December 2017, and then for the female VTuber agency whose first generation debuted in May-June 2018.In December 2019, Hololive will launch Cover's male agency, " Holostars, and music label INoNaKa, and merged them into a unified brand, Hololive Productions.
HISTORY
2016-19: Beginnings and growth
Cover Inc. was founded on June 13, 2016 by Motoaki "Yagoo" Tanigo, an entrepreneur who developed game characters with Sanrio at content company Imagineer and launched various startups Cover was initially an augmented reality (AR) and virtual reality (VR) software and received funding from incubator firms Tokyo VR Startups and Recruit.
At the end of March 2017, the company unveiled a technology demo of a program that enables real-time avatar motion capture and interactive two-way live streaming. According to Tanigo, the idea for the "virtual idol" agency was inspired by other virtual characters such as Hatsune Miku; Kizuna Ai, who started the virtual YouTuber craze in 2016, was likely an inspiration.
On September 7, 2017, Cover debuted its first VTuber, Toki no Sora, using the company's avatar capture software; on December 21, the company released an iOS/Android smartphone app that uses AR camera technology to view live streams of virtual characters called hololive" was released. on April 5, 2018, Cover removed the app's AR feature and replaced it with a tool that maps the user's facial movements to an animated avatar in real time. This update enabled auditions at home using the iPhone X. The first generation of Hololive VTubers debuted in May and June 2018, followed by the second generation in August and September. in December 2018 and April 2019, a group dedicated to Let's Play, " Hololive Gamers" debuted.

It will also work with Chinese-speaking volunteers to translate Hololive's videos and begin releasing original content for the Chinese market. on May 17, it began permanent talent auditions in China and Japan.

Disturbances and Suspension in Taiwan
In September 2020, Coco Kiryu and Haato Akai each mentioned Taiwan when discussing their YouTube channel analysis. Their remarks angered mainland Chinese viewers, and on September 27, Cover Inc. issued a statement in Japanese, English, and Chinese. In it, the company accused the talents of "making insensitive remarks about certain nationalities" and "leaking confidential information about YouTube channel analysis. Each was suspended for three weeks.
In a separate Chinese-only statement posted earlier on Bilibili, Cover said Coco and Hart's remarks did not reflect its policy toward China and reaffirmed its support for One Belt One Road and its commitment to doing business in China. Three days later, the company issued another statement addressing the discrepancy in messages, saying that Billibili's statement was issued "out of a desire to meet the needs of our audience." Cover apologized for the confusion and announced changes to the procedure for issuing regional statements and the formation of a committee to prevent similar incidents On October 19, both parties resumed distribution activities as scheduled.
The six members of Hololive China retired on separate dates at the end of 2020 after "bilious harassment, reporting, and an apparent rush to boycott" the group.
Business Description
Hololive Production, together with the VTuber office "Nijisanji" under Anycolor, dominates the corporate VTuber market as of 2022. By "leveraging the entertainment value of streaming and J-Pop idol groups," the company operates on a scale not possible for independent VTubers, and uses its funding to produce merchandise, produce music, and hold 3D live events, etc. As of 2021, Cover has raised three major rounds of funding fundraising:
* August 2017 - Raised 30 million yen (US$280,000) in seed funding from venture capitalists Mizuho Capital, TLM, and angel investors.
* June 2018 - 200 million yen (US$1.9 million) Series A round from GREE Ventures, OLM Ventures, and Mizuho Capital.
* May 2020 - Raised 700 million yen (US$6.6 million) from various sources including Hakuhodo DY Ventures and SMBC Venture Capital.
While many of Hololive's streamers work full-time, others stream on the side. According to data collection site Playboard, the all-time top earners of superchat are channels run by Hololive talents: Urja Lucia, Coco Kiryu, Pecola Usuda, and Marin Hosho. Others such as Minato Aqua, Amane Kanata, and Yukika Ramy are in the top 10 all-time.
By September 2020, Hololive's channels had a total of over 10 million subscribers on YouTube and another 10 million subscribers on the Chinese platform Bilibili. as of November 2021, Hololive had over 50 channels on YouTube with On October 22, English VTuber Gawr Gura became the first Hololive member to reach 1 million YouTube subscribers, and on January 17, 2021, he became the first Hololive member and the second VTuber to reach 1 million subscribers. On January 17, 2021, he became the first Hololive member and the second VTuber after Kizuna Eye to reach 2 million subscribers. On July 4, 2021, Gaur Gula became the first VTuber to reach 3 million subscribers on her YouTube channel, surpassing Kizuna Eye's 2.97 million on June 30.
According to data analysis firm Steam Hatchet, Usada Pecora is one of the top five most popular female streamers worldwide by viewing time, ranking fourth, third, and first in 2020, 2021, and 2022, respectively.
According to Tanigo, Hololive's primary viewers are males in their mid-teens to mid-30s, with Japanese fans more interested in gaming, while international viewers are particularly anime fans. The international viewers of Hololive are primarily from North America and Asia, especially Southeast Asia.
CEO Motoaki Tanigo was named one of the "Top 20 Entrepreneurs in Japan" in the January 2022 issue of Forbes Japan.
Studio
Cover Corporation invested 2.7 billion yen to build a new motion capture studio. The studio is equipped with state-of-the-art motion capture and recording equipment. The recording equipment is capable of music industry-standard audio capture. With an area the size of 10 tennis courts, the studio will be the largest Vtuber office in the world.
