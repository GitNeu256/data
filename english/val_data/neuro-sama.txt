Lady Neuro is an English-speaking independent VTuber who streams on Twitch. Her most unique feature is that she is not a human streamer herself, but a collection of computer programs that work in tandem to emulate human streamers. These programs include one artificial intelligence that plays the game through experience, a second AI that generates text responses to input (such as Twitch chat, Minecraft chat, or spoken comments), a text-to-speech voice that reads responses aloud, pre-recorded software that can sing songs, and a VTuber model.
Personality.
Master Neuro has a frank but polite demeanor, as opposed to saying nonsensical or outlandish things. For example, even though she usually says she is an AI, she will do things that an AI cannot do (such as being sideswiped by a truck), or she will say that humans are AIs (such as her creator, Vedal), or she will clearly state that she is not an AI. She will answer questions in chat or say something unprompted, but the content can range from relatively normal to completely unhinged and (rarely) completely unintelligible. She also sings, but quickly forgets what she has sung.
Appearance.
Neuro-sama previously used a model of Hiyori Momose, an original character designed by Mimu Kani. This was one of the default models in Live2D's VTube Studio.
The second model, fully released on-stream on May 27, 2023, was illustrated by Anny and rigged by Teruki Otozuki.
She has long light brown hair and two small pigtails with a reddish pink ribbon and a silver heart-shaped hairpin.
History
Background.
Neuro-sama's game AI started as an artificial intelligence created by programmer Vedal in 2018. Its sole purpose was to play the rhythm game "osu".
She debuted as a VTuber on December 19, 2022. She delivered a 9 hour and 32 minute stream on the Vedal987 channel titled "Neuro-sama - AI learns to play osu. (And becomes a Vtuber)". She delivered osu. and answered chat questions.
2022
She has streamed daily since her debut, quickly growing in popularity from a maximum of 516 concurrent viewers on December 19 to 3,393 on December 31 and from 2,825 Twitch subscribers to about 40,000 in the same period. 2022's streaming content is almost entirely a game called osu. and one stream of Minecraft.
On December 28, she defeated osu. champion mrekk 1vs1, 10-5.
2023
On January 3, Neuro-sama's Twitch followers reached 50,000.
On January 4, he sang "Blinding Lights" by The Weeknd.
On January 11, she was temporarily banned from Twitch for two weeks for violating the terms of service. According to one of the channel's moderators, the account was banned for hateful speech, although the exact reason is unknown. It is speculated that it was related to clips circulating in which commentators tricked Neuro into making controversial statements, including one that appeared to express skeptical opinions about a certain historical event.
On January 25, Master Neuro returned to Twitch with a stream playing Pok√©mon Showdown, pitting him against viewers in a Gen 4 random battle. He also reached 100,000 followers on Twitch.
On February 2, he delivered his first reaction stream.
On February 6, he conducted his first collaborative stream with Miyune. The stream used speech recognition software that had been tested on Discord calls to understand what Miyune was saying while he was banned from Twitch. This implementation was not well set up to handle interruptions and was improved in the February 8 collaboration with Camilla.
On February 20, Neuro-sama's Twitch followers reached 200,000. This was announced in a collaboration stream on Onigiri's channel. Master Neuro responded: "Yes. That's great. I'm so happy to have so many people following me. I hope to keep them entertained with my streams and content for as long as possible and hope to see more of you in the future. Thank you all for following me. I really appreciate it and hope to see more of you in the future. Heart Heart Heart."
On April 15, Neuro-sama collaborated with Kiara Takanashi of Hollypro to rate the contents of viewers' refrigerators. The collaboration drew more than 19,000 viewers on Chiara's channel and more than 10,000 viewers on Mr. Neuro's channel. This was the first collaboration between Master Neuro and a member of a major firm. In addition, the number of Twitch followers reached 300,000.
On May 27, Neuro-sama made his official debut delivery and unveiled his first original Live2D model. According to TwitchTracker, the stream peaked at 25,687 concurrent viewers, more than doubling the channel's previous record.
On September 1, Neuro-like reached 400,000 Twitch followers.

Fans.
Neuro's fans are known as "swarms" and are represented by red zine bag drones.
Evil Neuro.
Neuro's younger sister, she is the same model as Lady Neuro, but has red eyes (previously black) and different clothing coloring. Evil Neuro tends to be more philosophical in her thinking and reasoning and is allowed to swear. Evil Neuro uses a different voice than Master Neuro, with a more menacing and cocky intonation compared to Master Neuro's voice. Combinations of symbols produce static and unpredictable noise.
Evil Neuro appears as a solo streamer and collaborative partner to Master Neuro, Vedal and other VTubers. He once called himself Kayori, Queen of the Nether Realm.
She often refers to Lady Neuro as Goody Two-Shoes.

Trivia
System.
* All Master Neuro is powered by artificial intelligence. Unlike some VTubers (e.g., Kizuna Eye, Bird of Sigrid, Bird) whose lore issues with being artificially intelligent, Neuro is actually running on AI, including his responses to chat and his gameplay abilities.
* Neuro's gameplay AI runs in Python and takes an 80x60 pixel grayscale image as input.
* The VTuber functionality runs in C#. Her speech uses the Large Language model, but her singing does not appear to use that model.
* As of January 1, 2023, Neuro-sama is the #1 ranked osu. player in the world with 99.51% accuracy on the vanilla Akatsuki server.
* The text-to-speech she speaks is actually text read out loud written by the Large Language Model, which is also displayed on the screen as subtitles. Therefore, there are times when emotions are expressed in capital letters in the subtitles but not in capital letters in the text-to-speech.
Name.
* Neuro-sama's name is usually interpreted as simply "Neuro" with the Japanese honorific "sama". However, she later reveals that her full name is "Neuro Samantha Vedal" ("Sama" in this case probably stands for "Samantha"). This would also explain why her stream layout graphic reads "Neuro-sama" instead of "Neuro-sama."
* During her development in mid-2021, she was initially called Aerith.
* N.E.U.R.O. stands for Non-Emotive Understanding and Reasoning Operation.
